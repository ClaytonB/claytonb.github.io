Proof — Throughput & Cost

Summary highlights below. Choose Details for the math, or More info for the broader overview.

Myth #1
“Pods can’t match subway throughput.”
  • Pods @ 2/s × 2 pax = 14,400 pax/h/guideway
  • Trains: ~1,000 pax × 20/h = 20,000 pax/h/line
  • Network scaling: loops distribute demand; add lanes by adding loops.
Myth #2
“Cost per passenger‑km is higher.”
  • Capex: ~$1M/km vs ~$1B/km (order‑of‑magnitude delta)
  • Energy: no intermediate stops; regen braking; lower mass per passenger
  • Ops: autonomous 24/7; modular maintenance; high utilization

Detailed Assumptions & Calculations (Preview)

Guideway speed: 30–60 km/h nominal • Headway: 0.5–1.0 s effective (convoy control) • Pods: 2 seats

Throughput formula: pax/h = (pods/sec × 3600) × seats × occ

Example: 1 pod/sec × 3600 × 2 × 0.8 = 5,760 pax/h per guideway (conservative). Higher with 2 pods/sec or multiple loops.

Capex model: Elevated modular guideway + ports ≈ CAD ~$1M/km (target), scalable; compare to subway CAD ~$1B/km incl. tunneling.

“But it will cost more per passenger‑km.”

That’s the second myth. The combination of ultra‑low capex, continuous flow, and high utilization changes the math.

Low Capex

Targeting ~$1M/km guideway, modular nodes; factory‑built for rapid install.

High Utilization

Continuous operations, point‑to‑point; no long station dwells or off‑peak deadweight.

Energy Efficiency

Lower mass per passenger, regenerative braking, and smooth profiles reduce kWh/pkm.

Scalable Network

Loops add capacity like lanes; demand spreads across nodes instead of one bottleneck corridor.
